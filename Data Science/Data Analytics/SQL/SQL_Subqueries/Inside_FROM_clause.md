
<div class="track_article_contents__9JJFV"><h1 class="track_title__g20mM">Suquery Inside FROM Clause</h1><hr><div class="track_body__GeGQu"><h2><span>Subqueries in the FROM Clause of SQL</span></h2><p dir="ltr" style="text-align: start;"><span>Subqueries, or nested queries, are powerful tools in SQL that enable you to create more complex and specific queries by embedding one query within another. While subqueries are commonly used in WHERE and SELECT clauses, they can also be utilized within the FROM clause to manipulate data or generate derived tables.</span></p><h4><span>Syntax of Subqueries in the FROM Clause</span></h4><p dir="ltr" style="text-align: start;"><span>The syntax for a subquery within the FROM clause is as follows:</span></p><blockquote><p dir="ltr"><span>SELECT column1, column2, ...</span></p><p dir="ltr"><span>FROM (SELECT subquery_columns FROM your_table WHERE conditions) AS alias</span></p></blockquote><p dir="ltr"><u><b><strong class="GFGEditorTheme__textUnderline">Example Scenario</strong></b></u></p><p dir="ltr"><span>Let's consider a scenario where we have two tables: '</span><b><strong>employees'</strong></b><span> and '</span><b><strong>salaries'</strong></b><span>. The '</span><b><strong>employees' </strong></b><span>table stores information about employees, including their IDs and departments. Meanwhile, the '</span><b><strong>salaries'</strong></b><span> table contains salary information for each employee.</span></p><p dir="ltr"><u><b><strong class="GFGEditorTheme__textUnderline">Objective</strong></b></u></p><p dir="ltr"><span>We want to retrieve the average salary for each department.</span></p><p dir="ltr"><span>Implementation with Subquery in the FROM Clause</span></p><blockquote><p dir="ltr"><span>SELECT department, AVG(salary) AS avg_salary</span></p><p dir="ltr"><span>FROM (</span></p><p dir="ltr"><span>    SELECT e.department, s.salary</span></p><p dir="ltr"><span>    FROM employees e</span></p><p dir="ltr"><span>    JOIN salaries s ON e.employee_id = s.employee_id</span></p><p dir="ltr"><span>) AS sub</span></p><p dir="ltr"><span>GROUP BY department;</span></p></blockquote><p dir="ltr"><br><b><strong>Explanation</strong></b></p><p dir="ltr"><span>1. </span><b><strong>Inner Subquery</strong></b><span>:</span></p><ul><li value="1"><span>The inner subquery fetches the necessary data from the </span><b><code><strong>employees</strong></code></b><b><strong> </strong></b><span>and </span><b><code><strong>salaries</strong></code></b><b><strong> </strong></b><span>tables by joining them based on the </span><b><code><strong>employee_id</strong></code></b><span>.</span></li><li value="2"><span>It selects the </span><b><code><strong>department</strong></code></b><b><strong> </strong></b><span>column from the </span><b><code><strong>employees</strong></code></b><b><strong> </strong></b><span>table and the </span><b><code><strong>salary</strong></code></b><b><strong> </strong></b><span>column from the </span><b><code><strong>salaries</strong></code></b><b><strong> </strong></b><span>table.</span></li></ul><p dir="ltr"><span>2. </span><b><strong>Subquery in FROM Clause:</strong></b></p><ul><li value="1"><span>The outer query treats the result of the inner subquery as a derived table ('</span><code><span>A</span></code><b><code><strong>S sub'</strong></code></b><span>) within its FROM clause.</span></li><li value="2"><span>It uses the derived table to perform operations on the retrieved data.</span></li></ul><p dir="ltr"><span>3. </span><b><strong>Outer Query</strong></b><span>:</span></p><ul><li value="1"><span>The outer query calculates the average ('</span><b><code><strong>AVG'</strong></code></b><span>) salary for each department from the derived table.</span></li><li value="2"><b><code><strong>'GROUP BY department'</strong></code></b><span> groups the data by department, allowing the </span><code><span>AVG</span></code><span> function to compute the average salary for each unique department.</span></li></ul><p dir="ltr"><span>4. </span><b><strong>Final Output</strong></b><span>:</span></p><ul><li value="1"><span>The output displays the department names along with their respective average salaries.</span><br></li></ul><p dir="ltr"><br><span>Benefits of Using Subqueries in the FROM Clause</span></p><ul><li
